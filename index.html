How are these numbers calculated? ▼
1
Hours saved — manual hrs/year minus digital hrs/year across all active modules.
Hours saved = Σ (manual − digital) per module
2
FTE equivalent — hours saved ÷ 1,800 (45 hrs/wk × 40 working weeks, after leave & public holidays).
FTE = hours saved ÷ 1,800
3
Est. cost savings — FTE × avg HR salary × 12. Represents the labour cost of time freed from admin — not headcount reduction.
Cost savings = FTE × salary × 12
Payroll & statutory — ~6 min/employee/month for calculation and payslips, plus a fixed monthly block for submitting to 4 gov portals (i-Akaun, PERKESO, e-PCB, HRDCorp).
Leave — ~5 min/employee/month covering leave requests, MC verifications, and balance updates.
Claims — ~12 min/employee/month assuming 1–2 claims each, covering collection, policy check, approval, and reimbursement.
Attendance — ~10 min/employee/month for timesheet collection, OT classification under EA 1955, and pre-payroll reconciliation.
Performance — ~2 hrs/employee × 2 review cycles/year, covering form distribution, score collation, calibration, and filing.
Recruitment — ~4 hrs/hire for JD writing, job posting, CV screening, interview coordination, and offer letter.
Onboarding — ~3 hrs/new hire for contract prep, document collection, statutory registration, and orientation.
Training — ~2 hrs/employee/year for scheduling, attendance tracking, and HRD Corp claim submission.
Core HR & records — ~6 min/employee/month for P-file updates, document filing under the 7-year Employment Act requirement, and HR letter generation.

Penalty averages reflect real-world Malaysian compliance incidents and include interest compounding, backdated correction costs, and professional fees — not just the base statutory fine.
