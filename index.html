How these numbers are calculated
1
Hours saved — for each selected module, manual hrs/year minus digital hrs/year, summed across all active tasks.
Hours saved = Σ (manual − digital) across selected modules
2
FTE equivalent — hours saved ÷ 1,800 (Malaysian working hours/year: 45 hrs/week × 40 working weeks, net of leave & public holidays).
FTE = hours saved ÷ 1,800
3
Est. cost savings — FTE × avg HR salary × 12. This is the cost of time reclaimed from admin — not headcount reduction. Your team redirects that time to hiring, retention, and culture.
Cost savings = FTE × salary × 12
Time estimates per module
Payroll & statutory — ~6 min/employee/month + fixed portal overhead across 4 gov portals (i-Akaun, PERKESO, e-PCB, HRDCorp). Sublinear — fixed cost spread across more employees as headcount grows.
Leave — ~5 min/employee/month: applications, manager routing, balance updates, MC verification. Linear.
Claims — ~12 min/employee/month: receipt collection, policy check, approval chain, reimbursement. Linear.
Attendance — ~10 min/employee/month: timesheet collection, OT classification under EA 1955, reconciliation. Slightly superlinear above 100 ppl.
Performance — ~1.5 hrs/employee/cycle × 2 cycles/year: form prep, review sessions, calibration, documentation.
Recruitment — ~4 hrs/hire: job posting, CV screening, interview coordination, offer letter, rejection comms.
Onboarding — ~3 hrs/new hire: contract prep, statutory registration, payroll setup, orientation. Driven by hires/year.
Training — ~2 hrs/employee/year: training needs analysis, scheduling, attendance tracking, HRD Corp claim submission.

Non-linear scaling: Payroll is sublinear (fixed portal overhead). Attendance is mildly superlinear above 100 ppl (error compounding). Leave and claims are linear. Penalties are capped at statutory maximums.

BrioHR Estimates are indicative. Penalty figures are statutory maximums per KWSP, PERKESO & LHDN.
